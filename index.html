FAQ

What inspired this?

I moved. A short detox sounded like a good way to bring focus during the adjustment.

Why remove these three things for a month?

For the better part of a decade I’ve trained myself into giving small rewards for small tasks. I wake up every morning, plan the day or excercise, and then reward myself with coffee. Through the day, I complete various tasks, and reward myself with checking random internet feeds. At the end of the day I grab a drink or two for a job well done. I wake up the next day, and I have my coffee. And so life marches on, and you get the idea.

Individually, these rewards are harmless, even necessary. As Pavlovian reactions, I'm not so sure. So, I’m curious — what happens when I remove the easy ways that I reward myself for every day accomplishments? Will my focus and productivity improve? Will my relationships change? Will healthier decisions be easier to make, physically and mentally? How much money will I save?

What's the Internet Firehose?

Any social service with a scrolling feed that I habitually check - Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, etc. They're kinda like the junk food of social interaction. High calorie count (time suck), low nutritional value (shallow interactions).

What's okay to use?

Messenger services that are meant for one on one or small group conversation - Facebook Messenger, Whatsapp, Snapchat, Slack, etc. - and email.

How do I participate?

First, email me. I'd love to have your contact info to see how it went at the end of the November.

Second, swear off coffee, soda, tea, and booze. Uninstall anything with a feed (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Swarm, etc.) and only use messengers (Facebook Messenger, Whatsapp, Snapchat, etc.) or email to interact with friends and family. Enjoy the detox.
